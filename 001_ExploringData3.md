# **Use Case 3: Identifying Exceptional Students**

### *(Range, IQR, Boxplot, Standard Deviation, Z-Scores)*

**PDFs:** 1.05, 1.06, 1.07, 1.08

## **Scenario**

The university wants to identify **top performers and at-risk students** fairly.

---

## **Concepts Used**

* **Range** â†’ overall spread
* **Interquartile Range (IQR)** â†’ middle 50%
* **Standard Deviation (SD)** â†’ typical distance from mean
* **Z-score** â†’ relative position

---

## **Score Summary**

* Mean = 70
* SD = 10

---

## **Z-Score Formula**

[
z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma}
]

### Example

Student score = 90
[
z = \frac{90 - 70}{10} = +2.0
]

**Interpretation**

* Student is **2 SD above mean**
* Clearly exceptional performer

---

## **Boxplot (Outliers & Spread)**

**What Boxplots Reveal**

* Median location
* IQR (spread of typical students)
* Outliers (extreme high / low scores)

ðŸ“Œ **Important exam insight:**
